# Digital Forensics & Incident Response (DFIR) Interview Questions

## What Does DFIR Stand For?

Answer:

DFIR stands for:

Digital Forensics and Incident Response.

It combines forensic investigations with cybersecurity incident handling.

---

## What is Digital Forensics?

Answer:

Digital Forensics is the process of collecting, preserving, analyzing, and reporting digital evidence.

The goal is to determine:

- What happened
- How it happened
- When it happened
- Who was involved

---

## What is Incident Response?

Answer:

Incident Response is the process of identifying, containing, eradicating, and recovering from cybersecurity incidents.

The goal is to minimize damage and restore operations.

---

## What Are the Phases of Incident Response?

Answer:

1. Preparation
2. Identification
3. Containment
4. Eradication
5. Recovery
6. Lessons Learned

---

## Why is Documentation Important During an Investigation?

Answer:

Documentation helps investigators:

- Preserve evidence
- Track actions taken
- Build timelines
- Support reporting

Accurate documentation is critical during investigations.

---

## What is Evidence?

Answer:

Evidence is information collected during an investigation that helps determine what occurred.

Examples:

- Log Files
- Network Traffic
- Emails
- Hard Drives
- Screenshots
- Memory Dumps

---

## What is Chain of Custody?

Answer:

Chain of custody is the process of documenting how evidence is collected, handled, stored, and transferred.

Its purpose is to preserve evidence integrity.

---

## What is an Indicator of Compromise (IOC)?

Answer:

An IOC is evidence that may indicate malicious activity.

Examples:

- Suspicious IP Addresses
- Malicious Domains
- File Hashes
- Registry Changes
- Unusual Processes

---

## What is Log Analysis?

Answer:

Log analysis is the review of system and application logs to identify suspicious activity and security events.

---

## Why is Timeline Analysis Important?

Answer:

Timeline analysis helps investigators understand:

- What happened
- When it happened
- The order of events

Timelines are commonly used during incident response investigations.

---

## What Information Can Be Found in Network Traffic?

Answer:

Network traffic may reveal:

- Source IP Addresses
- Destination IP Addresses
- Domains
- File Transfers
- Connections
- Suspicious Communications

---

## What Tools Are Commonly Used in DFIR?

Answer:

Examples include:

- Wireshark
- Volatility
- Autopsy
- FTK
- Splunk
- Velociraptor

---

## What Would You Do if a User Reported a Suspicious Email?

Answer:

I would:

1. Collect information about the email.
2. Review sender information.
3. Examine links and attachments.
4. Determine whether the email is malicious.
5. Escalate or contain the threat if necessary.
6. Document findings.

---

## What is Malware Analysis?

Answer:

Malware analysis is the process of studying malicious software to understand:

- Behavior
- Capabilities
- Impact
