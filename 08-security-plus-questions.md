# CompTIA Security+ Interview Questions

## What is CompTIA Security+?

Answer:

CompTIA Security+ is an entry-level cybersecurity certification that validates foundational knowledge in cybersecurity concepts, networking, risk management, incident response, cryptography, and security operations.

It is one of the most recognized certifications for beginning a cybersecurity career.

---

## Why is Security+ Important?

Answer:

Security+ demonstrates that an individual understands fundamental cybersecurity concepts and is prepared for entry-level security roles.

Many government and Department of Defense (DoD) positions recognize or require Security+.

---

## What is the Principle of Least Privilege?

Answer:

The Principle of Least Privilege means users should only have the minimum permissions necessary to perform their job responsibilities.

This reduces the risk of accidental or malicious actions.

---

## What is Authentication?

Answer:

Authentication is the process of verifying a user's identity before granting access to a system.

Examples include:

- Passwords
- Fingerprints
- Smart Cards
- Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)

---

## What is Authorization?

Answer:

Authorization determines what resources or actions an authenticated user is allowed to access.

Authentication verifies identity.

Authorization determines permissions.

---

## What is Encryption?

Answer:

Encryption converts readable data into an unreadable format to protect confidentiality.

Only authorized users with the correct key can decrypt the data.

---

## What is Hashing?

Answer:

Hashing converts data into a fixed-length value that is used to verify data integrity.

Unlike encryption, hashing cannot be reversed.

Examples:

- SHA-256
- SHA-512

---

## What is Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)?

Answer:

Multi-Factor Authentication requires users to provide two or more forms of verification before gaining access.

Examples:

- Password
- Authenticator App
- Fingerprint
- Security Key

---

## What is a VPN?

Answer:

A Virtual Private Network (VPN) creates an encrypted connection between a user's device and a remote network.

VPNs help protect data when using public or untrusted networks.

---

## What is Social Engineering?

Answer:

Social engineering is the manipulation of individuals into revealing sensitive information or performing actions that compromise security.

Examples:

- Phishing
- Pretexting
- Baiting
- Tailgating

---

## What is Ransomware?

Answer:

Ransomware is malware that encrypts a victim's files and demands payment to restore access.

Organizations should maintain backups and incident response plans to reduce the impact of ransomware attacks.

---

## What is Defense in Depth?

Answer:

Defense in Depth is a layered security strategy that uses multiple security controls to protect systems and data.

Examples include:

- Firewalls
- Antivirus
- MFA
- Security Awareness Training
- Network Segmentation

---

## What is Risk?

Answer:

Risk is the likelihood that a threat will exploit a vulnerability and cause harm to an organization.

---

## What is Vulnerability Management?

Answer:

Vulnerability management is the process of identifying, assessing, prioritizing, and remediating security weaknesses before they can be exploited.

---

## Beginner Interview Question

Question:

Why are you studying for Security+?

Answer:

Security+ provides a strong foundation in cybersecurity principles and prepares me for entry-level roles in SOC, DFIR, and security operations.

It also supports my long-term goal of working in Digital Forensics and Incident Response.

---

---

## Interview Tips

- Understand concepts rather than memorizing definitions.
- Be prepared to explain real-world examples.
- Relate Security+ topics to your hands-on labs and GitHub projects.
- Show curiosity and a willingness to continue learning.
